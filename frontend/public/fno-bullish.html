F&O Bullish Trend Scanner Backtest — Two-exit PnL

Two exits (disappear + 15:15) 15:15 only
Scanner: MA + ADX + MACD (15-min) Entry = first-appearance + 5 min Exit 1 = disappearance + 5 min Exit 2 = 15:15 IST

Each contiguous run where a symbol appears in the 15-minute Bullish Trend scanner becomes one simulated long trade on the front-month future. Entry is taken 5 minutes after the first scan the symbol appeared in (a.k.a. LTP at first_scan + 5 min), and two exits are measured: (1) 5 minutes after the symbol first disappears from the scanner later that day, (2) a hard 15:15 IST session close. Each re-appearance within the same day generates a fresh trade . Quantity is the current 1-lot size of the future.

Read it as a head-to-head. Exit 1 is the scanner's own "trend-broken" signal — how fast the move fades matters. Exit 2 is the dumb "hold-to-EOD" baseline. If Σ(Exit 1) > Σ(Exit 2), the disappearance signal is adding alpha over time-stops; if reversed, the disappearance is exiting too early.
—
First / Last scan: 15-min scanner slots the symbol was present in Disappear: first scan where it was absent after the run Entry: 1-min open at first_scan + 5 min Exit 1 / Exit 2: 1-min open at disappear + 5 min / 15:15 IST PnL ₹: (exit − entry) × 1 lot size
